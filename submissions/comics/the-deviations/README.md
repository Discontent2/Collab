# The Deviations Submission Intake

**Repo Status:** Collaborator Intake / External Contribution / Non-Canon  
**Project:** Meridian Prime  
**Newspaper:** The Anomalist  
**Funnies Section:** The Deviations  
**Owner Review Required:** Yes

The Deviations is the single-panel gag cartoon section of *The Anomalist*, an ordinary newspaper published inside the weird sci-fi world of Meridian Prime.

This folder is for intake drafts, collaborator submissions, unreviewed gag panel concepts, and art prompts related to The Deviations.

Everything placed here begins as:

```text
External Contribution / Non-Canon / Unreviewed / Requires Owner Review
```

## Core Idea

The Deviations turns serious Meridian Prime pressure into tiny disposable jokes. It should feel like a tired cartoonist drew one impossible thing in the newspaper margin because the universe had become too large to complain about directly.

The newspaper is ordinary. The world around it is not.

## Allowed Pressure Echoes

Submissions may joke around:

- public anxiety
- World Works corporate care language
- Hydropolis bureaucracy
- scientific overconfidence
- route pressure
- misclassification
- old machines waking up
- false sanctuaries
- Antisapian signal weirdness
- Aeonolacertian everyday normalization
- microscopic dread
- cosmic indifference

## Protected Mystery Guardrail

Submissions may create rumor, pressure, false civic panic, bad cartoon interpretation, or public misunderstanding.

Submissions must not define, reveal, overwrite, or solve protected mysteries, including:

- the Core
- Dry Port truth
- Dry Gate truth
- gateway mechanics
- Contact Frame mechanics
- protected route topology
- Ildi’s restricted truth
- Conjugate Chimera truth
- Rob Holliday’s death-chain
- the NCI assassin
- Book One answers

## House Style

The Deviations should usually be:

- single-panel
- clean-crude
- heavy black border
- thick black outlines
- minimal backgrounds
- boxy potato-bodied figures
- wide circular eyes with tiny pupils
- joke-first, not lore-first

The best punchlines are small sentences that make the picture worse.
