## Creature Relationship To Aeonolacertians

A D20-generated creature may relate to Aeonolacertians through:

### 1. Pressure Reading

Aeonolacertians interpret the creature by what it reveals about weight, route stress, threshold behavior, silence, or old damage.

Example:

```text
A harmless burrower becomes a serious route sign because its tunnels collapse under careless machine weight.
```

### 2. Guest-Status Behavior

The creature may react differently to calm approach, lowered tools, quiet engines, or non-threatening body language.

Example:

```text
The animal allows passage only when travelers stop advancing directly and wait at the threshold line.
```

### 3. Route-Name Tradition

The creature may have an Aeonolacertian route-name that is poetic, pressure-based, or cautionary.

Example:

```text
Aeonolacertian route-name: The Small Mouth That Tests the Floor
```

### 4. Misread By Outsiders

Human crews, World Works, or other factions may misclassify the creature because they ignore Aeonolacertian pressure logic.

Example:

```text
World Works labels it a nuisance species; Craton route-keepers treat it as a warning that the ground has stopped consenting to heavy loads.
```

### 5. Moral Pressure Instead Of Combat

The encounter may test whether travelers treat living pressure as obstacle, warning, witness, or guest.

Example:

```text
The creature blocks a machine path not because it is hostile, but because its nesting site marks a sinkfield edge.
```

## Risk Rules

### Default Risk

Aeonolacertian-adjacent creature material should usually begin at:

```text
Canon Risk: Moderate
Spoiler Visibility: Subtext Only
```

Reason:

```text
Aeonolacertian material often touches route pressure, Craton gates, cultural law, species biology, and protected geography.
```

### Raise To Protected Mystery Risk If

Raise risk to **Protected Mystery Risk** if the creature:

- explains how a gate works
- confirms a true route
- defines protected topology
- reveals Craton hidden mechanics
- establishes final Aeonolacertian origin
- explains the World Maw as fact
- connects directly to Dry Gate, Dry Port, Contact Frame, Core, or Book One answers

### Keep Lower Risk If

Keep risk **Low to Moderate** if the creature only:

- creates atmosphere
- acts as threshold fauna
- has an Aeonolacertian route-name
- is interpreted through pressure culture
- causes etiquette tension
- appears near a route without explaining the route
- supports visual worldbuilding without solving mysteries
