## One-Paragraph Summary

The **Shrineclad War-Krab** is a goat-sized, shell-backed sanctuary-associated creature that nests in industrial ruins and dresses itself in discarded machine plates, broken road signs, torn armor housings, cable rings, and cracked tool casings. Though it looks battle-ready, even dragon-like from a distance, it is usually gentle and defensive. Its strange low call can trigger memory flashes in nearby people without explaining them, making it valuable, frightening, and politically dangerous to factions that want control over testimony, trauma, route history, or old sanctuary rumors.

## Visual Description

A heavy crustacean-like body with six thick walking limbs, a domed shell, hooked side-claws, and a blunt head tucked beneath layered plating. Its natural shell is pale stone-gray with red, amber, and charcoal mineral streaks, but most individuals are almost hidden beneath scavenged industrial armor. Some wear half-circular shield plates over their backs, making them resemble tiny armored dragons or dinosaur-era war beasts from old mural art.

Its sensory feelers are long, flexible, and wire-like. They drag across metal, concrete, shell, and ground, reading vibration through ruins. When threatened, the creature lifts its plated body, spreads its claws, and rattles the machine scrap across its shell like a tiny war standard.

## Habitat

Most often found around:

- industrial ruins
- abandoned route stations
- sanctuary edges
- broken machine yards
- old repair bays
- collapsed shelter corridors
- shrine-like spaces hidden inside wreckage

It prefers places where people once rested, repaired equipment, or left offerings. It is not necessarily a sanctuary creature in the protected-mystery sense. Safer framing: it is **sanctuary-associated gentle fauna** or a **witness-described rest-site species**.

## Behavior

The Shrineclad War-Krab is a slow, deliberate scavenger and guardian-adjacent animal. It gathers metal scraps and broken machine pieces not to build weapons, but to protect its soft shell seams and mark its territory. It often arranges scrap into half-circles around sleeping areas, water bowls, old tool benches, or quiet corners.

Its vibration feelers let it sense footsteps, engines, heavy machines, and tremors. It reacts badly to careless stomping or loud machinery, but responds calmly to slow movement, lowered posture, and quiet work.

Its call is the strangest trait: a hollow bell-like vibration produced by rubbing shell plates and scrap fragments together. People who hear it may briefly remember an old road, a lost work site, a companion’s voice, a childhood machine sound, or a battle they never personally saw. The creature does **not** explain these memories. It only stirs them.

## Relationship To People

The Antisapian community has a different name for it: **The Soft Alarm**. This name treats the creature as a warning that arrives gently before danger hardens.

Other groups interpret it differently:

- Local workers see it as a good sign near safe rest places.
- World Works may classify it as a salvage contaminant.
- Scavengers may steal its shell-scrap and anger it.
- Antisapian communities may treat its call as emotional weather or memory-pressure.
- Aeonolacertian route-keepers may interpret its behavior as a sign that a place should be approached quietly.
- Factions may fight over it because its call can stir testimony, guilt, or disputed route memories.

This creature works best as a **faction conflict object** rather than a monster. Different groups want different things from it:

- One faction wants to capture it for memory-trigger research.
- Another wants to protect it as sanctuary fauna.
- A corporate team wants to erase records of where it appears.
- A traveler hears its call and remembers something useful but incomplete.
- A dinosaur-informed Aeonolacertian warrior refuses to fight near it because the creature has marked the ground as quiet territory.
- A “dragon battle” scene is defused when the creature’s call makes both sides hesitate.

It is excellent for comics or games because its silhouette is readable: armored crab, scrap shell, bell-call, tiny war-dragon energy.

## Encounter Use

Possible encounter patterns:

- The party enters an industrial ruin and sees what looks like a small armored dragon crouched in scrap.
- A faction battle is about to begin, but the creature’s memory-call causes everyone to pause.
- Players must move through a ruin without loud steps or heavy machine vibration.
- The creature has nested around an important object, but removing it by force risks panic and injury.
- Two groups argue over whether it is sacred fauna, corporate property, or dangerous evidence.
- In a side-scroller, it acts as a moving shield hazard that blocks attacks but can be calmed by silence.

## Weakness / Non-Combat Handling

Best handled through quiet and patience.

Safe approaches:

- lower tools and weapons
- stop engines nearby
- move slowly
- avoid stepping on arranged scrap circles
- offer clean metal fragments away from the path
- let it retreat under cover
- do not imitate its memory-call
- do not pull plates off its shell

It is not built for fast attack. Its defense is emotional confusion, rattling intimidation, shell armor, and social consequence. Hurting one near a rest-site may make locals refuse aid.
