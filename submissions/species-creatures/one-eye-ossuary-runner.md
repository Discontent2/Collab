## One-Paragraph Summary

The **One-Eye Ossuary Runner** is a gaunt, quadrupedal, skull-headed route creature recognized by its oversized golden central eye, pale bone-like cranial shell, exposed tendon-cable musculature, and long grasping forelimbs. It is usually described as alien, undead, or laboratory-made by frightened witnesses, but intake framing should keep those claims unverified. In safer Meridian Prime terms, it may be a misclassified scavenger-runner, a wet-lab escape rumor, or a naturally strange organism that learned to survive around abandoned medical, industrial, and salvage corridors. Its stare is more dangerous socially than physically: people panic, misreport it, hunt it, sell fake parts, or mistake it for proof of hidden systems.

## Visual Description

A lean, low, fast creature with a skull-like head, one large amber-gold eye set in the center of the brow, and a wide toothy mouth that can open farther than expected. Its body appears partially stripped: pale bony plates over the skull, shoulders, forearms, and hips, with dark sinew, cable-like tendons, and wet organic fiber visible beneath. The hands are long and hooked, useful for gripping broken flooring, lab grates, tunnel ribs, and cliffside debris. A small golden glow may pulse inside the chest or throat when it breathes, giving the creature a lantern-like interior.

## Habitat

Likely encounter zones include:

- abandoned laboratories
- medical wash corridors
- ossuary-like maintenance tunnels
- route salvage yards
- false sanctuary edges
- cracked service basements
- wet industrial ruins
- dark culverts and drainage understructures

It prefers shadowed passageways where bone-colored surfaces, old ceramic panels, and exposed utility ribs let it blend into wreckage.

## Behavior

The One-Eye Ossuary Runner is nervous, swift, and intensely observant. It freezes when directly lit, then relocates with sudden bursts when attention breaks. It scavenges mineral residue, protein scraps, small carrion, fungus, and discarded medical or industrial gels. The large eye is believed to be adapted for low light, reflection reading, or threat detection, though no intake file should define its biology as confirmed.

It may stalk travelers at a distance, not to attack, but to learn whether they are a threat, a food source, or a path-opener. When cornered, it opens its mouth, spreads its hooked forelimbs, and emits a dry rasping alarm that makes it seem much larger than it is.

## Relationship To People

People almost always classify it badly.

Common rumors and bad records include:

- escaped alien experiment
- undead route pet
- child-sized bone demon
- lab-made scout animal
- contaminated skeleton organism
- false sanctuary guardian
- proof of hidden research

Safer intake framing: these are **rumors**, **misclassified records**, or **witness claims**, not established truth.

Black-market sellers may offer fake golden eye lenses, skull chips, and “lantern bones” supposedly harvested from the creature. Most are glass, carved ceramic, or ordinary animal remains.

Possible encounter patterns:

- A route crew finds one staring from inside a broken medical cabinet.
- A child insists the “gold-eye skull dog” led them out of a dark service tunnel.
- A corporate report labels it a contamination threat without evidence.
- A black-market stall sells fake eye-lenses as route charms.
- A false sanctuary ruin contains scratch marks and pale skull glimpses, making travelers think something is guarding the site.
- The creature follows characters because their lights flush out smaller prey.

## Weakness / Non-Combat Handling

Best handled without pursuit.

Safe approaches:

- keep distance
- lower harsh direct lights
- leave an escape route
- avoid cornering it in tunnels
- do not chase it into unstable corridors
- use food diversion rather than weapons
- treat the golden eye as a startle feature, not proof of hostile intelligence

It is fast and frightening, but not necessarily aggressive. Panic causes most incidents.
