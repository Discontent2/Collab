## One-Paragraph Summary

The **Mist-Ridge Needlecoil** is a long insectile snake-like ridge creature that lives in high mountain fog zones, cave mouths, understone cracks, and wind-cut ledges. Its body is a flexible ribbon of chitin plates, many tiny climbing legs, and buried defensive spines that remain hidden until pressure, weather, or fear wakes them. Aeonolacertian route-keepers sometimes read its movements as a weather sign, not because it predicts the future, but because it reacts early to pressure shifts, water movement, wind saturation, and unstable stone.

## Visual Description

A snake made from black-green beetle plates and fog-wet mountain stone. Its body is long and narrow, with hundreds of small hook-like climbing legs tucked beneath overlapping armor. The head is wedge-shaped and insectile, with pale whisker-like feelers hanging from the mouth and cheek ridges. When threatened, its back splits into rows of thin mineral-dark needles, giving it the look of a crawling thorn branch or animated mountain crack.

## Habitat

High ridges, cloud fog zones, cave mouths, understone tunnels, wet cliff faces, mountain drainage cuts, and old ledges above route corridors.

## Behavior

The Mist-Ridge Needlecoil is a climber and pressure-feeler. It moves along vertical stone with hidden leg-motion and feeds on lichen mats, cave insects, small ridge scavengers, mineral films, and carrion caught in ledge cracks. Before storms, rockfall, or sudden fog surges, groups may emerge and crawl across exposed stone in the same direction.

## Relationship To People

Travelers fear it because stepping on a hidden Needlecoil can trigger buried spines. Climbers and route crews respect it because its movement often means the ridge is becoming dangerous. Aeonolacertian route-keepers may watch its body direction, spine posture, and retreat behavior as mountain etiquette.

## Story Use

Environmental warning, mountain-route omen, climbing hazard, Aeonolacertian-adjacent pressure creature, fog-scene visual icon, and non-combat encounter that rewards observation.

## Encounter Use

A route crew sees several Needlecoils crossing the trail and must decide whether to stop before rockfall. A cave entrance seems safe until the walls begin moving. A guide refuses to continue because the ridge has not agreed to carry them.

## Weakness / Non-Combat Handling

Do not step on loose stone without checking. Lower posture, avoid looming, move slowly sideways, avoid loud stomping, wait if multiple Needlecoils cross the route, use poles to test fog-covered cracks, and never pull one from a crevice.
