## One-Paragraph Summary

The **Split-Lantern Tyranoslug** is a dog-sized, two-headed roadside molluskoid associated with containment leaks, trash ecologies, and post-occupation debris corridors. Though soft-bodied at its core, it carries a muscular collar, plated neck folds, and a forward posture that gives both heads a blunt predatory silhouette. Each head can project a narrow laser-like guide beam from a deep-set light organ. The beam is not a weapon; it is a scanning, signaling, and luring display. It often appears only after a sanctuary has already been found, which has caused desperate travelers to misread its lights as proof of a second refuge ahead.

## Visual Description

A low, heavy slug-body with a broad traction foot, scavenger-hardened hide, and twin necks rising from one armored shoulder mass. The two heads have squared snouts, thick brow ridges, and false-face markings. Pale membrane fans open around the throat and cheek lines. When it raises both heads, thin red or amber beams shine forward through dust, fog, or rain like survey lasers. Its skin is oil-dark gray, bruised red, and roadside mineral-stained.

## Habitat

Roadside trash ecologies, abandoned lay-bys, shoulder ditches, dump-fed edge biomes, ruined rest stops, and contaminated margins near old containment transport routes.

## Behavior

The creature is an opportunistic omnivore that feeds on rot, fungal mats, trace minerals, carrion scraps, and residue films on old plastics or metal. It may cling to larger animals for height and mobility. Its twin heads sweep independently: one watches terrain while the other tracks movement.

## Relationship To People

It can accidentally help lost travelers by moving toward safer ground, water runoff, or animal paths. It also causes panic when its beam-lines are mistaken for official route markers or shelter beacons. Antisapian-adjacent readings should remain local interpretation, not protected signal truth.

## Story Use

False sanctuary lure, roadside encounter fauna, signal-culture mood creature, companion-adjacent nuisance, and a visual icon for mixed hope and bad interpretation.

## Encounter Use

Use it when red beam-lines appear in fog after a sanctuary scene, when a route grazer carries one like a living hood ornament, or when travelers must decide whether a light is guidance, lure, or meaningless animal display.

## Weakness / Non-Combat Handling

Dim harsh lights, avoid surrounding it, do not grab or split the heads by force, offer damp ground access, redirect it with food scraps or mineral salt, and never treat beam-lines as official route guidance.
