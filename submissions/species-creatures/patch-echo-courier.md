## One-Paragraph Summary

The **Patch-Echo Courier** is a human-sized industrial-adapted ribbon creature associated with Worldskin test patches, scrap corridors, and failed corporate field zones. At rest it looks like a cable-thin, pale, flexible body draped through debris, but when not directly observed it can rear, loop, brace, and wear discarded frames in a way that creates the eerie impression of a cyborg velociraptor-human mutant. This is why many bad records describe it as a lab-made raptor person. In truth it is a strange, mostly harmless route-finder whose movements often mark the safest way across unstable treated ground, until a corporation decides to improve, brand, weaponize, or exploit it.

## Visual Description

Its true body is long, ribbon-like, and muscular, with slick skin broken by segmented industrial-looking ridges, fiber seams, and cable-sheath textures. The head is narrow and wedge-shaped, with clusters of vibration feelers around the mouth and jawline. Small gripping nubs and foldable support limbs along the body let it climb, brace, and hold scavenged material. When unobserved, it threads itself through broken harnesses, braces, maintenance frames, suit torsos, warning poles, and test-rig scraps, creating a startling part-human, part-velociraptor, part-cyborg silhouette.

## Habitat

Worldskin test patches, industrial treatment zones, abandoned field labs, maintenance corridors, treated soil yards, contaminated work camps, and scrap-littered transit edges.

## Behavior

The Patch-Echo Courier moves only when unobserved. When watched directly, it freezes, slackens, or goes limp like discarded hose, dead belt material, or collapsed test fabric. When attention breaks, it repositions with astonishing speed. Its vibration feelers read ground stress, machine rhythm, footsteps, and structural tremor. If one is cornered, nearby Couriers may gather silently from cracks, scaffolds, culverts, and scrap piles.

## Relationship To People

Experienced travelers may follow its movement trails through unstable ground, and route crews sometimes treat it as a sign of safer footing. Black markets sell fake Courier parts, fake feelers, fake route charms, and fake cyborg implants supposedly cut from the animal. Corporate capture, augmentation, training, branding, or behavior modification tends to turn a useful route creature into a dangerous unstable asset.

## Story Use

Traveler aid with eerie conditions, Worldskin ecology detail, corporate propaganda mascot, misclassification victim, black-market rumor creature, and moral warning about institutional improvement.

## Encounter Use

A traveler crosses a dangerous test patch by following strange repositioned silhouettes. A corporate poster shows a smiling cartoon Transit Helper while the real creature is shy and eerie. A field crew thinks one mutant is stalking them, then finds a whole herd encircling them silently.

## Weakness / Non-Combat Handling

Do not chase it directly. Do not flood it with spotlights. Avoid surrounding one individual. Give it escape lines. If crossing dangerous ground, observe where it was, not where it is. Do not trust corporate upgraded versions.
