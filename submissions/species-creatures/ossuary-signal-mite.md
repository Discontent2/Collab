## One-Paragraph Summary

The **Ossuary Signal Mite** is a fingernail-sized biomechanical pseudo-organism found near sanctuary edges, seep-pools, cracked basins, and false-sanctuary ruins. Each individual resembles a tiny pale skull fused with insect hardware and wet shell fragments. In colonies, they can make broken places look alive, safe, or recently maintained. Authorities often misclassify them as sensor grit, lab residue, or harmless machine parasites, but they are better understood as tiny lure-hazards whose mechanical mimicry can draw people toward unsafe refuge sites.

## Visual Description

Each mite has a rounded skull-like head-case with hollow eye pits, a tiny jaw ridge, and a smooth pale dome marked by dark seams. Beneath the casing are delicate swimmer-legs and folded finlike paddles. Their backs carry bead-sized metallic nodules and ribbed plates that look more manufactured than natural. Colonies appear as pale skull-specks drifting in black water, tiny clicking mask-faces in cracks, or dead indicator lights suddenly behaving like living beads.

## Habitat

Sanctuary edges, false sanctuary ruins, shallow seep pools, drainage basins, wet lab wreckage, cracked cistern floors, broken medical or industrial wash channels, and seasonal puddles beside ruined shelter sites.

## Behavior

The mite is aquatic or semi-aquatic, active in shallow water films, pooled runoff, and stone basins. In dry seasons, colonies retreat into damp cracks, pipe seams, hollow hardware, and porous ruin stone. Sound-sensitive membranes make them respond to voices, footsteps, tool clinks, and distant machinery. When stimulated, colonies produce synchronized clicks, whines, and tap-patterns that resemble active panel relays, lab instruments, automated doors, or service tones.

## Relationship To People

Authorities often misclassify the mites as inert biomechanical residue, failed sterilization contamination, decorative mineral growth, harmless filtration mites, or dead sensor dust. Their two opposing names show the split: **Blessing Flea** for people who think they signal water or shelter, and **Hush-Teeth / The Small False Mercy** for those who know they gather where hope has gone wrong.

## Story Use

Hazard, false sanctuary lure, tiny unforgettable encounter, lab-ruin atmosphere species, misclassification victim, and wet/dry seasonal warning organism.

## Encounter Use

A shallow basin full of pale dust begins moving when someone speaks. A ruined sanctuary edge hums like working systems, but the sound is only thousands of mites clicking in wet cracks. A false refuge appears active because colonies gather around dead panels and broken lights.

## Weakness / Non-Combat Handling

Do not follow machine-like sounds blindly. Avoid kneeling in shallow pooled water near abandoned shelter sites. Reduce vibration and back away slowly. Disturb colonies with redirected water flow rather than crushing them. Mark infested sites clearly for later travelers.

## Protected Mystery Notes

They may imitate active systems, but they do not reveal how sanctuaries work or prove the origin of sanctuary tech, biomechanical life, or any protected system. Any alien lab feeling should remain aesthetic, rumored, or misclassified.
