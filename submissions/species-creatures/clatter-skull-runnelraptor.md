## One-Paragraph Summary

The **Clatter-Skull Runnelraptor** is a dog-sized roadside scavenger with a lean velociraptor-like body, a skull-patterned face, and a habit of nesting inside, dragging, or wearing broken refuse cans, road bins, and cylindrical scrap housings. It thrives in trash corridors, abandoned lay-bys, and refuse-clogged runoff zones, where its lid-rattle calls and service-cart mimicry often make travelers mistake it for loose infrastructure, maintenance clutter, or someone moving around in the dark. Though eerie and widely treated as a plague scavenger or corpse-eater, it is mostly a nervous opportunist and a frequent victim of bad classification.

## Visual Description

A low, fast, long-tailed saurian scavenger with narrow hips, spring-loaded legs, grasping forelimbs, and a wedge skull shaped like a juvenile velociraptor crossed with a carrion bird. The face carries pale bone-like plating with deep black hollows that resemble a skull mask or warning stencil. Along the neck and shoulders are ragged trash-fiber tufts, torn plastic strips, wire tangles, and grime-dark quills. Many individuals drag or partially wear metal cans, road bins, or cracked waste cylinders like improvised armor or mobile hiding places.

## Habitat

Roadside trash ecologies, drainage ditches, abandoned route stops, littered industrial margins, culverts, runoff channels, refuse yards, collapsed service alleys, and scavenger camps after evacuation.

## Behavior

The Clatter-Skull Runnelraptor advances in short springy bursts, pauses, tilts its head, then darts again. It feeds on refuse, bone fragments, insects, fungus, food scraps, small carrion, and grease-rich organic residue. It uses mechanical mimicry to reproduce bin lids clapping, rolling can rattle, dropped tool echoes, cart-wheel chatter, and maintenance clanks.

## Relationship To People

Workers call it a nuisance scavenger. Children dare each other to look for skull cans at dusk. Camp cooks hate it because it raids food waste. Scavengers follow it because it finds rich trash pockets. It is often falsely recorded as a disease carrier, grave scavenger, corpse omen, escaped lab mutant, biohazard vector, or aggressive pest species.

## Story Use

Visually iconic scavenger, roadside hazard, jump-scare atmosphere creature, misclassification victim, illegal trade object, labor-culture nuisance animal, and scene-stealer in comics or side-scrollers.

## Encounter Use

A route crew hears bin-lid slams in the dark and thinks someone is stalking camp. A player reaches into a refuse drum and one launches out in panic. A sympathetic scene reveals the species is being exterminated because of false disease rumors.

## Weakness / Non-Combat Handling

Secure refuse and food waste. Do not corner one inside a container. Give it an escape route. Use light, not violence, to scare it off. Avoid responding to every metallic noise as if it were a threat.
