# Craton Geodeums

**Canon Status:** Owner-Approved Canon Concept
**Repo Status:** Collaborator Canon Reference / Do Not Contradict
**Primary Canon Source:** `Discontent2/meridian-prime/docs/worldbuilding/craton/craton-geodeums.md`
**Scope:** Craton / Geodeum architecture / public contest and judgment spaces
**Use Level:** Canon foundation; individual sites, rituals, and legal procedures require later owner review.
**Protected Mystery Status:** Does not define gateway mechanics, Dry Gate truth, the Core, Contact Frame mechanics, protected route topology, or Book One answers.

## Core Definition

A **Craton Geodeum** is a stone-governed public contest and judgment chamber where testimony, accusation, ritual passage, social legitimacy, witnessed consequence, and arena-style ordeals may be decided under Craton authority or Craton-adjacent custom.

Some Geodeums are colosseum-like public arenas.

Some Geodeums are trial-by-stone chambers.

The worst ones are both.

```text
A Craton Geodeum is where conflict stops being private and becomes stone.
```

```text
The crowd watches. The stone listens. Survival is sometimes mistaken for truth.
```

## Essential Identity

A Geodeum is not merely a crystal cave or decorated arena.

It is a bounded stone space where conflict becomes public, witnessed, and difficult to deny.

A Geodeum may decide or display:

```text
status
guilt
passage-right
social legitimacy
ritual standing
public accusation
champion authority
faction dispute
classification pressure
```

A Geodeum does not need to be morally right. It only needs to be recognized, endured, witnessed, and recorded by stone, crowd, custom, or authority.

## Primary Modes

### Arena Geodeum

The public contest mode: loud, performative, ceremonial, and crowd-facing.

Common uses:

```text
champion contests
status disputes
proxy faction contests
public proving grounds
crowd-pressure scenes
```

Common features:

```text
tiered seating
crystalline or mineral walls
marked contest circles
gate lifts
raised stone plates
ring platforms
resonance drums
stone chimes
old claw marks
impact scars
repaired crystal ribs
mineral dust underfoot
```

### Judgment Geodeum

The trial-by-stone mode: solemn, quieter, and often more frightening than the arena form.

Common uses:

```text
accusation scenes
truth ordeals
passage disputes
naming pressure
classification disputes
sentence chambers
witness confrontations
legal or ritual ordeals
```

Common features:

```text
standing stones
central slab or ring
resonant floor
witness dais
mineral growths that respond to contact, voice, impact, or ritual position
acoustics that make every word feel binding
pressure doors that open only after the rite concludes
stone channels that mark where each participant stood
```

## Additional Subtypes

```text
Threshold Geodeum: passage-right, border pressure, route legitimacy, or contested approach.
Reliquary Geodeum: older, ceremonial, archival, ancestral, or sacred in tone.
Pit Geodeum: harsher public ordeal form.
Crown Geodeum: elite or formal Geodeum for state-level, faction-level, or high ceremonial contests.
```

## Aeonolacertian Spatial Fit

Geodeums should not feel like human arenas with crystal decoration.

They belong to a world where Aeonolacertian and other non-human body plans exist in public space.

A Geodeum may account for:

```text
tail clearance
claw traction
low crouch lanes
sprint arcs
leap platforms
perch ledges
brace points
mineral ramps
vertical grip surfaces
circling body language
display structures under ritual lighting
```

This does not mean every Geodeum is exclusively Aeonolacertian, but Geodeum architecture should not erase saurian anatomy when Aeonolacertian use is relevant.
