# Craton Geodeums Reference

**Canon Status:** Owner-Approved Canon Concept  
**Collab Status:** Contributor Reference / Guardrail  
**Primary Canon Source:** `Discontent2/meridian-prime/docs/worldbuilding/craton/craton-geodeums.md`  
**Purpose:** Help contributors and collaborator-facing rooms use Craton Geodeums without turning them into protected-mystery explanations.

## Core Definition

A **Craton Geodeum** is a stone-governed public contest and judgment chamber where combat, testimony, accusation, ritual passage, social legitimacy, and witnessed consequence may be decided under Craton authority or Craton-adjacent custom.

Some Geodeums are gladiator colosseum arenas.

Some Geodeums are trial-by-stone chambers.

The worst ones are both.

Core line:

```text
A Craton Geodeum is where conflict stops being private and becomes stone.
```

Alternate public saying:

```text
The crowd watches. The stone listens. Survival is sometimes mistaken for truth.
```

## Geodeum Modes

### Arena Geodeum

Public, loud, performative, physically dangerous. Good for combat trials, champion contests, punishment spectacle, and faction disputes.

### Judgment Geodeum

Solemn, quieter, and often more frightening. Good for accusation, testimony, naming pressure, classification disputes, and trial-by-stone scenes.

### Threshold Geodeum

Tied to passage-right, border pressure, route legitimacy, or contested approach.

### Reliquary Geodeum

Older, more ceremonial, archival, ancestral, or sacred in tone.

### Pit Geodeum

A rougher punitive form where public punishment hides under the language of proof.

### Crown Geodeum

A formal elite Geodeum used for high-level ceremonial, faction, or state-level contests.
